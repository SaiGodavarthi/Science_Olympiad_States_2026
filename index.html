Strategic Rationale

The proposed event distribution is designed to maximize our team's total score by focusing effort where it produces the greatest competitive gain. Using the Brown Invitational results as a baseline, we identified events where Cumberland can realistically move into first place with targeted preparation, rather than spending excessive time on events where other teams already have a large advantage.

This strategy prioritizes high return-on-investment (ROI) events — meaning events where small improvements in preparation or execution could shift placements significantly. Events like Circuit Lab, Hovercraft, and Machines show strong potential for Cumberland to move from mid placements to first with additional study or optimization.

The team assignments align competitors with events where they already demonstrate relative strength or relevant skills. This reduces the learning curve and allows members to deepen expertise instead of spreading preparation across unfamiliar topics.

Some events — such as Bungee Drop where Barrington placed first — would require disproportionate effort to surpass. The strategy secures solid placements there while investing preparation time where it will actually lower our overall team score. Overall, this gives Cumberland the strongest statistical path to victory.
